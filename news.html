    The Cybersecurity Association of China (CSAC) has urged a security review of Intel products sold in the country, claiming the US semiconductor firm poses ongoing threats to China’s national security and interests. In a statement posted on its WeChat account, CSAC said that Intel’s major product quality and security management flaws indicate its extremely irresponsible attitude toward customers. CSAC is an industry body, but its allegations raise concerns about a potential security review and subsequent action by the country’s cyberspace regulator, the Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC). Last year, CAC banned products from Micron, citing national security risk. Significantly, this year Intel has secured orders for its Xeon processors from several Chinese state-affiliated agencies for AI applications, according to Reuters. This action marks the latest chapter in the ongoing trade conflict, which has seen US administrations ban Chinese-made hardware from domestic networks and impose export controls to limit China’s access to advanced computing technologies.
